### The Influence of The Castle of Cagliostro on Romancing the Stone

I was recently re-watching the classic 80’s movie [_Romancing the Stone_](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0088011/) (1984) and at some part I started noticing some striking similarities to one of my favorite movies; the Miyazaki classic [_The Castle of Cagliostro_](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0079833/) (1979). For example, Danny Devito’s little car…

![](/media/the-influence-of-the-castle-of-cagliostro-on-romancing-the-stone-0.jpeg)

<figcaption>1973 Renault 4 L</figcaption></figure>

looks remarkably similar to Lupin’s

<figure>

![](/media/the-influence-of-the-castle-of-cagliostro-on-romancing-the-stone-1.jpeg)

<figcaption>Fiat 500</figcaption></figure>

The characters also have a propensity for car chases and driving cross country. This by itself might be just a coincidence, but then towards the end of the movie, Michael Douglas’s character scales a wall to save the heroine…

<figure>

![](/media/the-influence-of-the-castle-of-cagliostro-on-romancing-the-stone-2.png)

</figure>

Which bears a striking resemblance (and narrative, actually) to the famous rooftop run in _The Castle of Cagliostro_.

<figure>

![](/media/the-influence-of-the-castle-of-cagliostro-on-romancing-the-stone-3.jpeg)

</figure>

This is just before Lupin’s mad run down the precipitous roof and prodigious jump; ending in a spectacular splat against a neighboring tower, the last which has a similar counterpart in _Romancing the Stone_ (when “Jack” slams in to the canyon wall trying to swing across).

This is all well and good, and could just be coincidence, but I think the quotation of _The Castle of Cagliostro_ in _Romancing the Stone_ is entirely intentional. You see, the director of _Romancing the Stone_ is Robert Zemeckis, a protege of Steven Spielberg who went on to direct [_Back to the Future_](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0088763/)  (1985) and [_Forrest Gump_](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0109830/) (1994) among others. As the DVD for _The Castle of Cagliostro_ claims, Steven Spielberg called it “one of the greatest adventure movies of all time.”

While it is a source of [speculation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Castle_of_Cagliostro) how much Spielberg was actually influenced by the Japanese director, it is reasonable to suppose that the film _was_ known and discussed by the directors. We may never know for sure, but it seems plausible that Zemeckis made some stylistic choices in his film to subtly impress his mentor in a way that no one would ever notice.
